Early Internship Program

The Early Internship Program involves six students that have completed the first year CS sequence an undergraduate internships as early as possible. In addition to sharpening and expanding their technical skills, an internship experience develops an awareness of how classroom concepts are converted into applications of value in today’s society. These type of opportunities, that allow students to experience real world problems and gain confidence in their ability to contribute in meaningful ways, are particularly helpful in recruiting and retaining women in CS.

Our partners in the IUSE SPARCS summer internship program include both external and internal (Siena) partners. In the summer, after their freshman year, six students are selected from the students who have completed the first-year CS sequence. They spend six weeks working on a real-world project for one of the internship partners. Siena CS faculty meet in advance with mentors from each organization to identify meaningful projects, keeping in mind that these students will be in the early stages of their CS education. The students meet regularly with their partner supervisor during the six-week period to discuss such things as project requirements, project management, technology platforms, evaluating tangibles, etc. For the external partner interns, a Siena CS faculty (the PI or a Co-PI on the grant) travel with the students to attend these meetings as needed to understand the details of the project. The students do the actual work in the CS department at Siena College where they can draw on the technical expertise and guidance of the Siena CS faculty. Because the students are reporting to an off-campus organization/supervisor, this additional involvement of CS faculty is useful for managing the smaller day-to-day issues and questions that arise due to the lack of CS maturity, having only completed the first-year sequence. For the internal (on Siena's campus) interns, the students work closely alongside a professional mentor who provides training, mentoring, and technical expertise. Regular lunches are organized for all the interns and Siena CS faculty. At these lunches, they share their internship experiences, successes, and challenges as well as discuss issues related to the workplace.

At the time of the summer internship, the students have completed three computer science courses covering introductory programming (primarily in Java, some Python), general CS topics, and web design. Students may use a portion of their internship time reviewing books and tutorials to learn specialized software tools and applications needed for their internship projects. Examples of the types of projects the students may work on include:

  • Researching and experimenting with new technologies.
  • Testing and evaluating software tools and applications.
  • Designing and creating web sites.
  • Prototyping user interfaces using tools like Flinto or Pop.
  • Requirements gathering
  • Writing programs to analyze data sets or massage data into proper format.
  • Developing databases using Microsoft Access.
  • Managing and maintaining computers and providing technical support to users.

The students receive a summer stipend for the work, and non-local students are also be provided with an additional stipend to cover campus room and board. Students may choose to earn Siena credits for the internship, although normal summer tuition fees apply.
