2016 Summer CSIS Experience

The summer CSIS experience will be run in Roger Bacon Hall on Siena's campus Monday-Friday August 29 - September 2 (moving in on Sunday August 28).  There will be morning and afternoon sessions each day, as well as some evening sessions.   If you will be living on campus for the Fall 2015 semester, you can simply move in to your regular dorm room a week early.  If not, we will put you up in a dorm for the week.  In either case, there is no cost to you for food or lodging.  In addition, if you live locally and would rather not live in the dorms during the program, that is fine too.

The goal of this program is to help you decide whether Computer Science is something you want to explore further (and if you do, we'll save a seat for you CSIS110 in the Fall or Spring).  You are not committed to anything beyond this week, however, and there are no grades to worry about.

So, what will we do during the week?  A combination of hands-on activities and guest presentations.  We will introduce 1-2 topics per day, Afternoons will be spent exploring how these topics are applied in our labs. Here is a list of potential topics (this list may change):

  • Robotics
  • Game Development
  • Big Data
  • Networks and Graphs
  • Models of Computation
  • Manipulating images and sounds
  • Programming in Java
  • Data sharing and transactions
  • Information storage and compression
  • Error detection
  • Searching for information
  • Encryption/decryption

In addition to the primary daily activities of learning about the field of Computer Science, we will also (mainly in the evening) offer faculty lectures, guest presentations about career opportunities, areas of research in computer science, and talks from our current advanced CS majors discussing their internships, career prospects, how to choose a major/minor, research work, etc.
