High School Dual Enrollment Program

A major component of the SPARCS program is to jump-start CS education in local high schools where it is not currently offered. We work closely with partnering high schools and cooperating math/technology teachers to start a dual enrollment program that offers an introductory computer science course for which their students can receive Siena College credit. Few NYS schools have a teacher with the formal CS background necessary to teach a rigorous, concepts-rich college level CS course (as opposed to teaching utilitarian skills in keyboarding and use of commercial software packages). Therefore, the key focus of this component of our program is educating cooperating teachers on the concepts and programming skills covered in Siena’s context-based Introduction to Computer Science course (CSIS110). We will do this through a two year program that pairs a college faculty member with a cooperating teacher to team-teach the course, with the goal of having the high school teacher gain the confidence needed to teach the course on their own starting in the third year of the course offering and beyond. We have connected this initiative to the early assistantship retention initiative, by providing stipends to Siena students who have completed the first-year CS sequence to work alongside Siena faculty at the high schools as student teaching assistants.

A slightly scaled-down version of this program has been funded internally by Siena to continue beyond the grant period.  High school teachers will work directly with a Siena Course Coordinator to assist them with teaching Siena's Introduction to Computer Science Course at their high school.  The internal funding will allow us to help all partnering schools long-term.  Interested in being a partner high school?  Contact our High School Course Coordinator, Prof. Jim Matthews.
