Early Assistantship Program

Each semester we engage students as paid classroom/lab assistants during their sophomore year. Just as early research experiences have shown positive results on retention of STEM majors, we hypothesized that we will see similar effects by engaging students as teaching assistants. Students build mentoring experience and confidence in their CS abilities. This experience offers students enrolled in the course an opportunity to receive more one-on-one attention, which may also improve overall recruitment and retention. In addition, we expect to see improved academic performance from the teaching assistants since they will be reviewing the material in-depth for a second time. Compared to a summer internship experience which includes 6 weeks of stipend or salary plus room and board for non-local students, the cost of a student teaching assistant is significantly less. As classroom/lab assistants, the students either assist a Siena CS faculty teaching Introduction to CS (CSIS110) at a high school through our dual enrollment program, or they assist a faculty teaching CSIS110 or Introduction to Programming (CSIS120) at Siena. They are supervised by the faculty member and their responsibilities are limited to assisting students with activity questions during lecture/lab time only.
